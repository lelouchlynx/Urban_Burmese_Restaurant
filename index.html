Signature Stories

From Eseence to Elegance On Your Plate

Scroll to follow each Burmese dish from raw ingredients to its plated moment.

Mohinga

Myanmar’s national fish noodle soup, rich with lemongrass and crisp toppings.

  1. Ingredients

    Fresh aromatics and fish form the base for a deep, savory broth.

  2. Cooking

    The broth slowly simmers until every note is balanced and fragrant.

  3. Final reveal

    Ladled over rice noodles and finished with lime and crisp fritters.

Laphet Thote

Iconic tea leaf salad with crunchy nuts, seeds, and bright lime.

  1. Ingredients

    Fermented tea leaves, toasted nuts, sesame, and fresh greens.

  2. Cooking

    Everything is gently tossed so every bite stays crisp and bright.

  3. Final reveal

    A bold, crunchy salad that’s sour, savory, and addictive.

Dan Pauk

Burmese-style biryani with saffron rice and tender chicken.

  1. Ingredients

    Fragrant rice, warm spices, and marinated chicken.

  2. Cooking

    Layered and steamed so every grain drinks in the flavor.

  3. Final reveal

    Golden, aromatic rice served as a celebratory centerpiece.

Ohn No Khao Swe

Creamy coconut noodle soup with a golden, silky broth.

  1. Ingredients

    Coconut milk, noodles, aromatics, and gentle spice.

  2. Cooking

    A slow simmer turns the broth velvety and aromatic.

  3. Final reveal

    Finished with crisp toppings for the perfect spoonful.
